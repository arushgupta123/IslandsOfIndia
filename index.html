The Islands of India

The Andaman and Nicobar Islands

  • Location & Geography:
    • Located in the Bay of Bengal, closer to Southeast Asia.
    • Made up of 572 islands, of which around 37 are inhabited.
    • It is divided into two groups: Andaman Islands (North) and Nicobar Islands (South)
  • Volcanic and hilly terrain, tropical rainforests.
  • Home to Barren Island, India’s only active volcano.
  • Surrounded by coral reefs and rich marine biodiversity.

Lakshadweep Islands

  • Location & Geography:
    • Located in the Arabian Sea, off the coast of Kerala.
    • Only 10 of the islands are inhabited.
    • Meaning "one hundred thousand islands" in Sanskrit.
  • Unique Features:
    • Known for pristine white sandy beaches and clear turquoise waters.
    • Rich in marine life, vibrant coral reefs, and lagoons.

Strategic Advantages to India from Islands

  • Defense:
    • Provide maintenance and surveillance stations for navy
    • Helps monitor seas, control chokepoints
    • Islands like the APJ Abdul Kalam, help making missile test pads
  • India might have found oil in the Andaman Sea
  • Economic
    • Islands can promote tourism
    • Islands can promote fishing and such industries
